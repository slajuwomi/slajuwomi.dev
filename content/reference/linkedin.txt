If you're looking at this and thinking, "Man, I guess some people make it", understand this:

1) LeetCode is hard for most people in the beginning

Problem solving in and of itself is a skill. 
Learning the algorithms and data structures takes time. 

Don't get discouraged if you cannot solve a problem. It just means that this is new to you and you should keep going.

2) You don't need to be a genius

Many of these problems were first solved by actual mathematicians, so it can seem like you need to be an intellectual or something.

Tbh, it's about pattern recognition. 
That's why we have 3 extensive roadmaps to help you with this, including:

- Blind 75
- NeetCode 150
- NeetCode 250

NeetCode 250 is the most beginner friendly and gets you feeling confident fast.

3) Don't underestimate consistency

A big reason why many people completely quit is because they can't stay consistent.

We get this a lot in our comments:

"My problem is that I am consistent for a bit and then I forget what I worked on a few days ago and end up quitting."

It's fine if you happen to miss a day. Life comes up. As long as you immediately get back to it, you are doing fine.

Don't use missing one day as a reason to become completely inconsistent.

4) You will never feel fully ready

A lot of people do endless problems, 600+, because they feel the more problems they do, the more prepared they are.

But a lot of times, this can be a way of avoiding doing the interview. 

If you can solve most medium problems that you haven't seen before, within 20 to 25 minutes, you can probably pass most interviews.


Most importantly, never, ever give up.

You'll be surprised at how fast you can start getting good if you just quit quitting.
